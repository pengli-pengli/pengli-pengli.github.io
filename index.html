Hi, there!

My name is Peng Li. I received my Ph.D. degree in 2021 from the Universitat Pompeu Fabra, Spain. I am currently a Junior Researcher at Center of Linguistics, University of Lisbon (CLUL), Portugal, funded by the Foundation for Science and Technology (FCT).

I used to work as a Juan de la Cierva fellow (2024-25) funded by the Spanish State Agency at the Basque Center on Cognition, Brain, and Language (BCBL), Spain, post-doc fellow in Psycholinguistics (2022-23) at the University of Oslo (UiO), Norway, and Lecturer (2013-17) of Japanese Studies at the University of Hefei (China).

My research interests include second language (L2) speech acquisition, multimodal phonetic training, phonetics and prosodic studies, and bilingualism. My research can be summarized in three lines

  • Speech and bilingualism. I focus on the crosslinguistic transfer between bilingual speakers' first and second languages.
  • Multimodal phonetic training. I develop various training techniques for L2 phonetic and prosodic learning, including hand gestures, visual feedback, tactile feedback, auditory training, etc.
  • Individual differences in SLA. I try to understand how cognitive and experiential factors affect the development of L2 speech acquisition.
